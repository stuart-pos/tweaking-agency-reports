Executive read

51/100 means roughly half your search-term governance is working, but more than a quarter of evaluated spend (~$1.15M of ~$4.2M) is on queries that never converted in-window — and the biggest dollar risk is not "bad keywords" but core product terms that convert at CPA well above target. That pattern matches your business note that account CPA is climbing without a confirmed root cause.

Do these three things this week, in order:

First, do not run a broad negation sweep on core GLP-1 terms (compounded tirzepatide, glp 1, cheapest tirzepatide, zepbound, etc.). 489 of 638 "underperforming" terms are core-relevant (~$785k spend flagged) — these are conversion/LP/intake problems, not traffic-quality problems. Route to /lp-auditor and /offer-auditor and validate intake CVR before blaming search terms.

Second, negate competitor and navigational waste that is clearly off-brand — top zero-conversion examples include coreagerx, yucca health, hims tirzepatide, weightrx, and competitor/review n-grams (trimrx, slimdownrx, com reviews). ~$1.15M non-converting spend (27.4% of term spend) — a large share is safe structural cleanup after human review.

Third, attach Search_Brand to your shared negative lists (only active campaign missing shared-list linkage).

What is NOT a problem: zero negative conflicts (ST-D08), zero repeated negative consolidation debt (ST-D09/D10), zero legacy +modified +broad negatives (ST-D11), no close-variant drift (Module 4 clean), no active PMax search-term leakage. Search_Tirzepatide_Semaglutide and Shopping_GLP-1_Subs already have campaign-level negatives.

No fresh peer audit reports existed in context at time of audit — upstream tracking and LP checks were flagged as needed (tracking audit subsequently completed 2026-06-09 at 84%). Score trend: baseline run — no prior score in log.

Diagnosis

The problem is at the Business + Conversion layer first, Traffic cleanup second. Core-intent queries are spending heavily and converting below the effective CPA ceiling (~$458 at 1.5× $305 campaign inline target), which explains climbing account CPA better than "we need more negatives." Competitor/navigational queries are real waste (~27% non-converting spend share) but are smaller than core-term inefficiency. Start with intake/LP and ad group tCPA alignment ($650 in account vs $450 strategic target), then run a scoped negation cycle on non-core competitor terms.
