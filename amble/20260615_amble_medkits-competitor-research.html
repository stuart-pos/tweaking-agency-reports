Executive summary

Amble’s survival kits compete in the prescription telehealth emergency-preparedness category — not OTC first aid. Closest Google Ads competitors are Jase Medical, The Wellness Company (TWC), OZIO, Runway Health, JetSet Meds, Wandr, ReadyMed, and Heads Up Medical. These brands dominate Search text ads and Shopping product carousels on queries like emergency antibiotic kit, travel emergency medical kit, and travel medicine kit online doctor.

Do not treat Shopping as generic first aid. The carousel mixes Rx kits with Adventure Medical and MyMedic OTC products ($33–$250). Segment by intent and use feed titles that signal physician-prescribed emergency medications.

Jase and TWC are the paid-search gatekeepers. Amble pricing is competitive with Jase Case ($290) and TWC Travel ($275) but above travel entrants (Runway à la carte, Wandr $199–$224, JetSet Weekender $195).

Scenario-specific kits are Amble’s edge. Eight use-case LPs map to long-tail Search — if LPs are fixed and Google Rx certification is confirmed. Before scaling: fix empty “What’s included” sections, replace NAD+ testimonials, and set kit-specific conversion values ($285–$945 AOV).

Google Ads policy

Certification required
Prescription drug services policy applies to all med kit campaigns
Confirm Google healthcare certification scope. Avoid drug generic names in ad copy — use condition-based language. Violations are egregious (suspension risk). Policy reference
  1. Confirm certification for prescription drug services (separate from GLP-1 if needed).
  2. Confirm LegitScript or equivalent (TWC displays prominently).
  3. Use condition-based copy, not azithromycin/doxycycline in headlines.
  4. LP disclaimers are strong — maintain on all kit pages.

How to market on Google — action list

  1. Treat med kits as a new product line with separate conversion values and CPA targets.
  2. Lead with Search + Shopping on Rx-intent keywords; use eight scenario LPs for message match.
  3. Fix LP content bugs and testimonials before scaling spend.
  4. Confirm healthcare certification; build Shopping feed with scenario-specific titles.
  5. Launch Just in Case + Mayday first; expand seasonal; conquest Jase/TWC/Runway.
  6. Exclude OTC first-aid intent from kit campaigns.
  7. Model unit economics per SKU — Panic Pack can absorb $150+ CPA; Just in Case needs tighter efficiency.
