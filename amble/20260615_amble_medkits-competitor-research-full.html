Executive summary

Amble’s survival kits sit in a prescription telehealth emergency-preparedness category — not the OTC first-aid market. Your closest Google Ads competitors are Jase Medical, The Wellness Company (TWC), OZIO, Runway Health, JetSet Meds, Wandr, ReadyMed, and Heads Up Medical. These brands dominate both Search text ads and Shopping product carousels on high-intent queries like emergency antibiotic kit, travel emergency medical kit, and travel medicine kit online doctor.

Key strategic implications:

  1. Do not treat Shopping as a generic “first aid kit” play. Google Shopping for these queries mixes Rx kits (Jase, TWC, OZIO) with OTC Adventure Medical / MyMedic products ($33–$250). Amble must segment campaigns by intent and use feed titles that signal physician-prescribed emergency medications, not bandages-and-gauze kits.
  2. Jase and TWC are the paid-search gatekeepers on preparedness and antibiotic-kit terms. Amble’s price points ($285–$945) are competitive with Jase Case ($290) and TWC Travel Kit ($275), but above travel-focused entrants (Runway à la carte, Wandr $199–$224, JetSet Weekender $195, ReadyMed $279).
  3. Scenario-specific kits are Amble’s differentiation. Most competitors sell one general antibiotic case plus small add-on SKUs (dental, UTI, cruise). Amble’s eight use-case kits (travel, viral, cold/flu, allergy/mold, radiation, field emergency, first aid + Rx) map to long-tail Search and dedicated landing pages — a structural advantage if LPs are fixed and certified for Google Rx policy.
  4. Before scaling spend: confirm Google Healthcare / prescription drug services certification (and LegitScript if applicable), fix landing page content bugs (empty “What’s included” sections, irrelevant NAD+ testimonials), and set kit-specific conversion values ($285–$945 AOV vs. current ~$200 GLP-1 default).

Google Shopping competitive patterns

Who buys Shopping placement for Rx-adjacent kits?

  1. Jase Medical — widest SKU coverage (Case, Go, Mission, Dental, UTI, Trauma)
  2. The Wellness Company — Travel + First Aid Emergency kits
  3. OZIO — Basic / Advanced / Pro tiers
  4. FSA Store — resells Adventure Medical (OTC) alongside emergency bundles

Typical Shopping titles that win impressions

  • Lead with use case + Rx signal: “Emergency Antibiotic Kit – 10 Essential Rx Meds…”
  • Include physician trust: “Doctor-Prescribed”, “Physician Prescribed”
  • Include scope: “30+ Conditions”, “Traveler’s Diarrhea”, “UTI”, “Dental”

Price bands in Shopping carousel (Rx kits)

  • Entry: $130–$200 (Jase Go, OZIO Basic, JetSet Weekender)
  • Core: $250–$350 (Jase Case, TWC Travel, ReadyMed, Heads Up, Amble Just in Case / Mayday)
  • Premium: $380–$650 (Jase Mission, JaseMedic)
  • Ultra: $945–$1,344 (Amble Panic Pack, OZIO Pro, Jase Max bundles)

Shopping strategy implication for Amble

  • Feed 8 SKUs with unique titles per scenario — do not collapse to one generic “Amble Med Kit.”
  • Use custom labels: rx_kit, travel, viral, prepper, premium.
  • Set aggressive negative keywords on Shopping campaigns: bandage, IFAK, tourniquet, OTC, refill only.
  • Consider Performance Max with asset groups per kit only after conversion tracking assigns correct values per SKU.

Google Ads policy — prescription drug services

Amble med kits require compliance with Google’s prescription drug services policy:

  • Certification required to promote online prescribing/dispensing in the US
  • Ads may run “Eligible (limited)” even when certified — restricted drug terms in copy/keywords
  • Landing pages must be fully functional, globally accessible, and match certification scope
  • Violations are egregious (account suspension risk)

Operational checklist before launch:

  1. Confirm Amble’s Google Ads healthcare certification status for prescription drug services (separate from GLP-1 campaigns if scope differs).
  2. Confirm LegitScript (or equivalent) — TWC prominently displays this; it’s becoming table stakes for trust and may affect approval.
  3. Avoid uncertified use of drug names in ad text (e.g., azithromycin, doxycycline) — use condition-based copy (“traveler’s diarrhea support”, “emergency antibiotic kit”).
  4. Ensure LPs include required disclaimers (Amble LPs already have strong ISI/disclaimer blocks).

Recommended Google Ads strategy

1. Positioning & message architecture

Primary value props (test in order):

  1. Physician-prescribed emergency medications shipped to your door — matches Jase/TWC category language
  2. Scenario-specific kits — “Built for travel / viral season / mold exposure / radiation preparedness” (Amble-only)
  3. 100,000+ patients + HSA/FSA eligible — trust + payment flexibility
  4. Flat price, no hidden fees, consult + shipping included — vs Runway’s consult + à la carte

Avoid:

  • Competing on “best first aid kit” or “trauma kit” without Rx qualifier
  • Fear-mongering beyond what competitors already use (match tone, don’t exceed)
  • Generic “survival kit” without medical/legal context

Summary: how to market these on Google

  1. Treat this as a new product line with its own campaign shell, conversion values, and CPA targets — not an extension of GLP-1 bidding.
  2. Lead with Search + Shopping on Rx-intent keywords where Jase and TWC already prove demand; use eight scenario LPs as the message-match weapon.
  3. Fix LP content bugs and testimonials before spending — you will lose to Jase/TWC on trust if medication lists are empty.
  4. Confirm healthcare certification and build Shopping feed with scenario-specific titles.
  5. Launch Just in Case + Mayday first, expand to seasonal kits, conquest Jase/TWC/Runway on brand and comparison terms.
  6. Exclude OTC first-aid intent from kit campaigns to protect CPA.
  7. Model unit economics per SKU — Panic Pack at $945 can absorb $150+ CPA; Just in Case needs tighter efficiency.
